SLC Tutor Checklist

A Framework for Effective Tutoring

1. Preparation

  • Review previous appointments & comments. Provides context and helps personalize the session.
  • Gather materials. Ensures a smooth and efficient session start (textbook, calculator, etc.).

2. Initial Contact

  • Welcome the student with a warm smile and use their name. Sets a positive, professional tone for the entire session.
  • Create a safe learning environment where it's okay to make mistakes. Reduces student anxiety and encourages active participation.

3. Assessment & Goal Setting

  • Ask for the assignment instructions. Ensures the session is aligned with the professor's expectations.
  • Use open-ended questions to check for understanding. Helps accurately diagnose student needs and their true starting point.
  • Set realistic and collaborative goals for the session. Manages expectations and provides a clear focus for the appointment.

4. Instruction & Guidance

  • Use scaffolding: start with a known concept to build confidence. Address foundational gaps before tackling more complex topics.
  • Use the Socratic method and have the student lead. Promotes critical thinking and independence by letting the student "hold the pen."

5. Practice & Feedback

  • Ask them to explain each step as they work. Verifies their thought process, not just the correctness of the final answer.
  • Verify understanding with a similar problem. Confirms the student can apply the concept independently.

6. Concluding the Session

  • Recap goals and what was accomplished. Reinforces learning and boosts the student's sense of progress.
  • Discuss next steps for independent study. Provides clear, actionable direction for the student to continue learning.
  • Promote word-of-mouth marketing. Encourage satisfied students to refer their classmates.

7. Post-Session Tasks

  • Send proof of attendance if needed.
  • Write a detailed, professional report. Provides a valuable record for instructors and future tutors.
  • Take your scheduled breaks. Prevents burnout and ensures you are ready for the next student.
